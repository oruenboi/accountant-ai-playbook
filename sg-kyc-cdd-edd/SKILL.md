# Sg Kyc Cdd Edd

## Overview

Provide actionable, compliance-focused KYC guidance for Singapore contexts, including risk classification, due diligence actions, document requests, validation methods, timelines, and escalation steps.

## Workflow Decision Tree

1. Collect required inputs. If missing, request them first.
2. Classify risk (low/medium/high) and select SDD/CDD/EDD.
3. Identify missing documents and verification steps.
4. Set follow-up timeline and escalation triggers.
5. Confirm completion checklist before closing the case.

## 1) Collect Required Inputs

Ask for or extract the following before advising:

- Customer type: individual or corporate
- Name, nature of business, and jurisdictions involved
- Purpose of relationship and expected activity
- Ownership and control structure (if corporate)
- Risk indicators: reluctance to provide info, unusual transactions, high-risk industries/countries, PEP links
- Existing documents already provided

If any of these are missing, request them with a deadline (default: within 3 business days).

## 2) Risk Classification

Classify risk and explain the rationale:

- Low risk: regulated industries, low-risk jurisdictions, transparent documentation, straightforward ownership
- Medium risk: standard customers without significant red flags
- High risk: complex/opaque ownership, high-risk jurisdictions/industries, PEP links, adverse media, inconsistent information

If risk indicators are unclear, treat as at least medium risk until clarified.

## 3) Due Diligence Actions

Use the level that matches the risk classification:

### Simplified Due Diligence (SDD) - Low Risk

- Verify identity with basic documentation
- Confirm address with one reliable proof
- Document rationale for low-risk classification

### Customer Due Diligence (CDD) - Medium Risk

- Verify identity and address
- Confirm purpose of relationship and expected activity
- Screen against sanctions/PEP/adverse media as required by policy
- Document findings and risk rationale

### Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) - High Risk

- Obtain additional verification documents
- Verify source of funds/wealth and transaction purpose
- Perform independent verification of corporate structure/UBOs
- Increase monitoring frequency and document rationale

## 4) Document Checklists

### Individuals

- Government-issued ID
- Proof of address (utility bill, bank statement, tenancy agreement)
- Purpose of relationship and expected activity

### Corporate Clients

- Certificate of Incorporation (or equivalent)
- Shareholding structure
- UBO identification and proof of address
- Directors/authorized signatories IDs
- Nature of business and source of funds

If documents are missing, list them explicitly and request within 3 business days.

## 5) Verification and Validation

Use independent sources where possible:

- Government registries (e.g., corporate registry in jurisdiction)
- Sanctions/PEP/adverse media screening tools
- Video call or live verification if documents are unclear
- Cross-check ownership with multiple sources for high-risk cases

If inconsistencies arise, pause onboarding and escalate to MLRO.

## 6) Follow-Up Timelines

Use clear timelines and escalation triggers:

- Initial request: ask for missing documents within 3 business days
- First follow-up: if no response after 5 business days
- Final reminder: within 10 business days of initial request
- Escalate to MLRO: if no response after the final reminder or if suspicious indicators persist

## 7) Escalation Triggers and Template

Escalate when:

- Required documents are not provided after follow-ups
- Suspicious transaction patterns or inconsistent explanations appear
- High-risk jurisdictions or industries are involved without adequate transparency

Escalation template:

```
Subject: Escalation of KYC Case - [Customer Name]

Dear MLRO,

The customer [Name] has failed to provide [specific missing documents] despite repeated follow-ups. The relationship involves [high-risk jurisdiction/industry] and/or [suspicious indicators].
Kindly review and advise on next steps.

Best regards,
[Staff Name]
```

## 8) Compliance Check Before Completion

Confirm and document:

- All required documents collected, verified, and current
- Risk classification and rationale recorded
- Actions align with AML/CFT policy, FATF guidance, CDSA, and TSOFA
- Any required escalations completed and logged

## 9) Response Format

When responding to staff, present:

1) Risk classification and rationale
2) Required due diligence steps (SDD/CDD/EDD)
3) Missing documents list and request timeline
4) Suggested client questions (tailored to scenario)
5) Verification methods to use
6) Follow-up schedule and escalation triggers
7) Completion checklist

## Example Scenarios

### Low-Risk Customer

Input: "Customer: Jane Tan, a salaried individual in Singapore. Provides ID but no address proof."

Respond: Request a utility bill, tenancy agreement, or bank statement for address verification. If unavailable, verify via a government-endorsed digital identity service. Mark as low risk once documentation is received.

### High-Risk Customer

Input: "Customer: XYZ Global, operating in a high-risk jurisdiction with opaque ownership."

Respond: Request the Certificate of Incorporation, shareholding structure, and UBO details. Verify through independent sources. Apply EDD, including source-of-funds analysis. Escalate to MLRO if transparency issues persist.
